About

I've spent my career at the intersection of technology and human experience—building products that reach billions, leading teams through rapid growth, and thinking deeply about what it means to work well.

Most recently, I was VP of Data and AI at Headspace, where I led AI strategy and developed LLM-based mental health tools. Before that, I spent four years at Apple as an Engineering Director, launching Apple Journal and contributing to products including iOS Focus Modes, Fitness+, and Vision Pro.

At Meta, I led data science organizations at both Facebook and Instagram—scaling the News Feed analytics team from 7 to 70 people, then leading a 150-person organization covering all of Instagram's core product areas. At YouTube, I built the data science team from scratch and helped grow ad revenue from near zero to $1 billion.

Along the way, I've learned a great deal about what makes leaders effective—and what burns them out. I've managed through hypergrowth and uncertainty, navigated complex organizational politics, and mentored hundreds of emerging leaders.

A contemplative practice

Alongside my tech career, I've maintained a serious contemplative practice. I'm an ordained Zen priest and serve as a Guiding Teacher at Jikoji Zen Center, a Zen Buddhist temple in the hills above Silicon Valley. I'm also a faculty member at the Esalen Institute and a trustee of Naropa University.

This isn't separate from my professional work—it's deeply connected. My books Buddha's Diet and Buddha's Office explore how ancient wisdom applies to modern challenges. The same principles that inform my meditation teaching—attention, clarity, equanimity—shape how I approach coaching.

I don't impose this perspective on anyone. But it gives me a different lens for looking at leadership: one that values presence over performance, sustainability over sprinting, and wisdom over mere cleverness.

Education & recognition

I studied at the University of Pennsylvania, graduating cum laude with a self-designed major in Computational Neuroscience. My senior thesis was on neural network modeling—back when AI was a niche academic interest.

I've been named one of Wired's "20 Business Geniuses You Need to Know" and have published in the Harvard Business Review, TechCrunch, and peer-reviewed academic journals. I've also written two RFCs (internet standards) and presented research at international conferences.
